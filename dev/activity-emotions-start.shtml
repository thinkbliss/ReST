Benefits

Practice the following steps when you are having negative thoughts or a stress reaction:

  • Learning to defuse negative thoughts significantly decreases anxiety and depression.1,2
  • Quick recovery from stress will enable you to be more be more focused and productive, and help you handle conflict and crises more effectively. This has enormous benefit in relationships and work.3

Instructions

Practice the following steps when you are having negative thoughts or a stress reaction:

  1. Notice: as soon as you notice you are having negative thoughts, or a negative emotional reaction, take a deep breath, and bring your attention to what you are thinking and feeling.
  2. Accept: rather than trying to ignore, resist or otherwise change your thoughts and emotions, try to accept them. It can help to imagine that you are a nonjudgmental, compassionate observer, narrating your experience.
  3. Narrate: name whatever you are thinking and feeling, "noting" your experience out loud or to yourself, e.g. "You’re feeling tense and angry and you’re thinking this isn’t fair.""

When you’ve finished, take a couple of slow, deep breathes, and return your attention to the task at hand or your next steps.
