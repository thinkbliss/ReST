Connect with Nature

Did you know that walking in the woods for as little as 15 minutes causes measurable physiological changes? A study in Japan found that a 15-minute nature walk led to a 16% decrease in the stress hormone cortisol, a 2% drop in blood pressure, and a 4% drop in heart rate.

Bottom Line: Go outside!

Get the Right Light

To remedy sleep woes, try to get some morning exposure to natural daylight, or strong light with blue tones. In the evening, shift to softer light with red and orange tones, and limit exposure to bright lights, including electronic screens, as you approach sleep.

Bottom Line: To brighten your mood, brighten your morning (and de-light your night).

x

Be the Boss … of your actions

We often let our emotions dictate what we do, waiting until we are confident or “in the right mood” before taking action. However, leading a fulfilling life requires that we repeatedly choose to do what is important to us, even if we feel like crap and would rather hide under the covers.

Bottom Line: Instead of trying to control your mood, control your actions, and your mood will eventually follow.

x

Get Out of Your Head and into Someone Else’s Shoes

To make a connection during a social or professional interaction, rather than trying to impress, focus on what the other person might be thinking or feeling. This will reduce self-consciousness, and allow you to dedicate your full attention to reading and responding to the situation.

Bottom Line: To connect with others, focus on them not you.

x

It Helps to Help

Did you know that altruism is one of the greatest buffers against depression? Doing something for someone else raises our own levels of hope, joy and happiness and helps us cope with difficult or traumatic events.

Bottom Line: Lifting someone else’s spirits will lift yours as well.

x

The Happiness Paradox

The media inundates us with the message that we’re supposed to be happy. This can contribute to feeling bad about feeling bad, and make us try to suppress, ignore or deny difficult emotions. Unfortunately, this only makes these emotions stronger and more persistent.

Bottom Line: If you want to be happier, accept negative feelings and remind yourself they will pass.
