Connect with nature

Spending time in nature improves attention, restores mental energy, relieves stress, reduces depression, anxiety and ADHD symptoms, and boosts the immune system.1,2,3,4 Nature works primarily by lowering stress. Physiological measurements of stress hormones, respiration, heart rate, and sweating indicate that even a short amount of time outdoors is calming and sharpens performance.1 A study in Japan found that a 15-minute walk in the woods led to a 16% decrease in the stress hormone cortisol, a 2% drop in blood pressure, and a 4% drop in heart rate.5 Even a view of nature, or looking at pictures of natural landscapes, has a positive effect. People with a window view of trees and grass have been shown to recover faster in hospitals, perform better in school, and display less violent behavior in neighborhoods where it’s common.1,6
